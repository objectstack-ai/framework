# What Can It Do?

This page answers one question: **"can the platform do the business feature I need?"** — in business language, with no code. Every capability below ships in the platform today, and the official **HotCRM** template (one-click install from the Marketplace) implements all of them end to end, so you can open a running app and click through everything you read here.

If you are a developer, the [Build sections](/docs/data-modeling) cover the same ground with full technical depth.

## Manage business data

Every kind of record — accounts, contacts, leads, opportunities, orders, tickets — is an **object** you can create and change at any time. Fields cover 40+ types: text, amounts with currency, dates, single/multi select with colored options, people pickers, star ratings, progress bars, addresses and map locations, images and file attachments, e-signatures, QR codes, tags, and auto-numbered document codes.

Two field families do work for you:

- **Formula fields** compute values automatically (*expected revenue = amount × probability*; *days until close*).
- **Roll-up fields** total child rows onto the parent (*an opportunity's product total updates the moment a line item changes*).

Records relate to each other: one account has many contacts and opportunities; an opportunity owns its product line items (edited inline as a grid, saved atomically); many-to-many and tree hierarchies are built in.

## See data the way each team works

The same records can be opened in **nine list views** — table, kanban board, calendar, gantt, gallery, timeline, map, tree, and chart — plus three form styles (simple, tabbed, multi-step wizard). Views are saved, named, and shared: sales reps drag deals across a *Pipeline* board grouped by stage, delivery managers plan the same data on a gantt, leadership reads it as charts. Filters support relative dates ("this quarter", "last 7 days" — 36 built-in tokens) so saved views stay current forever, and global search understands partial matches across objects.

## Keep data clean at the point of entry

Validation runs when records are saved, not at month-end: required fields, format checks, cross-field logic (*close date cannot be in the past*), and **state machines** that define which stage transitions are legal (*negotiation → won or lost; no skipping ahead*) — enforced by the runtime on every write. Public forms can be published to a URL for anonymous submission (web-to-lead), exposing only the fields you whitelist.

## Let processes run themselves

Automation flows are configured as visual diagrams and trigger five ways: when records change (with conditions that can compare old vs. new values), on schedules, relative to record dates (*30 days before contract expiry*), from user-launched wizards, or from external system calls. Inside a flow you can create and update records, send notifications (in-app, email, chat), branch on conditions, loop over record sets, wait for time or signals, and call external services.

HotCRM ships sixteen of these ready-made: lead auto-assignment and conversion, opportunity stagnation alerts and won announcements, quote generation and expiry reminders, contract renewal tasks, case escalation with SLA monitoring and CSAT follow-up, campaign enrollment, and task reminders.

## Approvals

Any step that needs a human decision becomes an approval node: eight ways to pick approvers (specific person, position, submitter's manager, team, department including children, a person named on the record, or a shared queue) and four decision modes — first response, unanimous, M-of-N quorum, and **group sign-off** where each group must independently approve. Records lock while pending, approval status mirrors onto the record for filtering, overdue approvals escalate automatically, and *send back for revision* returns the record to the submitter for another round instead of a hard reject. Every decision is permanently logged.

## Control who sees what

Access is layered, and the runtime enforces it on every request — including reports, APIs, and AI:

1. **Capability level** — which personas can create, read, edit, or delete each object, granted through permission sets (effective access is the union of everything granted).
2. **Data depth** — five scopes from *own records only*, through *own and reports* (management chain), *business unit*, *unit and below*, up to *organization-wide*.
3. **Record sharing** — declarative rules widen the baseline (*opportunities over $1M auto-share to the sales VP with edit access*), plus manual shares.
4. **Field-level security** — the same record shows different fields to different people (*cost price visible to managers only*); hidden fields are stripped server-side everywhere.

Organizations model business-unit trees and positions, with reporting lines driving the "my team's data" scopes. Every change is audited (who changed what, when, old and new values), and an explain facility answers "why can this person see this record?" layer by layer.

## Analytics that answer questions

Dashboards compose ~20 chart families — KPIs with period-over-period comparison arrows, bar/line/area trends, funnels, donuts, treemaps, tables and pivots — over centrally defined datasets, so a metric like "closed revenue" means the same thing on every chart. Dashboards take runtime filters (quarter, owner, source), and full-bleed TV-style display pages are supported for office screens. Reports come in tabular, summary, matrix, and joined forms, with scheduled email digests.

## AI that respects the rules

AI operates under the same permissions and audit trail as people. Ask questions in natural language and get answers **with charts**; let the AI create and update records or run actions (dangerous ones require confirmation, or route into a human-approval inbox); connect your own AI tooling — every deployment exposes its objects and actions as a governed MCP endpoint, so agents like Claude can operate the app you built. HotCRM includes a sales copilot (lead scoring, customer 360, email drafting, revenue forecasting) and a service copilot (case triage, live data Q&A).

## Connect everything else

Bulk import/export, generated REST APIs for every object, outbound webhooks on data changes, inbound triggers from external systems, ready-made connectors (Slack, generic REST), federation of existing external databases (query them in place — no migration), and a Marketplace of installable template apps. Interface text is translatable per language; HotCRM ships in four.
